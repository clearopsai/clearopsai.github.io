The four gaps that always appear

1. Clearance Tracking at Scale

At 50 people, someone in HR knows every clearance status by memory. At 200 people across 7 locations with different compartmentalization levels, clearance renewal cycles, polygraph schedules, and crossover eligibility become impossible to track manually. Missed renewals mean employees pulled from contracts mid-delivery.

Impact: Contract delivery disruptions, rebid risk, loss of institutional knowledge on active programs

2. Resource Allocation Across Compartmented Programs

Each classified program operates in its own silo by design. But leadership still needs to answer: "What is our overall utilization rate? Where are we overstaffed? Where are we leaving revenue on the table?" Answering that question across compartmented programs requires manual aggregation from systems that were never designed to talk to each other.

Impact: 8% to 15% revenue leakage, inability to forecast hiring needs accurately

3. The Recruiting Funnel Black Hole

Fast-growing defense IT firms process thousands of applications per year. Only a fraction are cleared or clearable. Tracking which candidates are in which stage of clearance processing, across which programs, at which locations, requires a level of operational visibility that most firms simply do not have. The result is recruiter productivity losses and candidate drop-off.

Impact: 20% to 35% longer time-to-fill for cleared positions compared to firms with automated tracking

4. Compliance Reporting Across Multiple Frameworks

DISA IL4, CMMC, NIST 800-171, agency-specific security requirements. Each framework has its own evidence requirements, audit cycles, and documentation standards. At scale, maintaining compliance across all of them simultaneously without a centralized visibility layer becomes a full-time job for multiple people.

Impact: 3 to 6 weeks of audit prep that could be reduced to days with the right automation

What the fix looks like at this stage

Weeks 1 to 2

Ops Mapping

Map every tool, data flow, and manual process across all locations and programs. Identify the top 3 to 5 visibility gaps by cost and risk. Produce a prioritized roadmap.

Weeks 3 to 8

Build the Visibility Layer

Connect the systems that matter most. Build dashboards for clearance tracking, utilization rates, recruiting funnel health, and compliance status. Automate the reports that leadership currently assembles by hand.

Ongoing

Scale with the Growth

As headcount grows and new contracts are won, the visibility layer grows with it. New programs, locations, and compliance frameworks get integrated as they are added.
